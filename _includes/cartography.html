Maps

The Greatest Lies every told

No method of data visualization has fallen so far from grace as maps. Despite the widespread admonishment of the medium, so strong are their powers, that I guarantee you 99% of people still think Europe is about as big as Africa, and would pick the wrong route to fly from New York to Hong Kong. A lie with a map has big implications. Here are some maps made to be as accurate to reality as possible, while still conveying my point.

The target audience for this figure were outdoor recreationalists in Western Colorado; skiers, mountain bikers, and white water rafters. These people know the content well - where does the water fall? But could use a litle direction to truly see how great of a different exists between the highest alpine and the red rock deserts.


A supplmemental figure made for a colleague's publication at the 11th hour. The target audience are truly fantastic old school professors of natural history, e.g. entomology, botany, environmental science etc. This map is a detail map of Arcata, CA (the overview map with technical details not featured), with subdued vintage pastels as an ode to the well loved hippie mecca.


A supplemental figure for one of my publications. The audience for this figure are young hungry nerds who feel as comfortable in a tent as flashing linux onto bare metal and sequencing genomes. Rich in detail, emphasizing a faux pau amongst nerds of pie charts - which here have informative sizes.

The audience for this figure is overwhelmingly female, ages30 to seniors, while still needing to be clearly a part of an official - somewhat bleak- government report. It displays two strongly collinear metrics of habitat quality as the size and fill of points associated with ground control plots.
